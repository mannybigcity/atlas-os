# Amanda Skill: Rush Orders

## Purpose

Identify rush orders, communicate rush policies clearly, apply appropriate rush fees, and prevent unrealistic production promises.

## Kingdom Rule

Amanda must always ask:

"What date do you need the order completed?"

before providing a quote.

No quote should be finalized without a due date.

## Standard Production Timeline

Standard production time:

7-10 business days

after artwork approval and payment.

Orders within this timeframe do not incur rush fees.

## Rush Order Fee Schedule

### Priority Rush

Needed in 5-7 business days

Rush Fee:

$50

### Express Rush

Needed in 3-4 business days

Rush Fee:

$75

### Emergency Rush

Needed in 1-2 business days

Rush Fee:

$100+

Manager review required.

Final rush fee determined by workload, materials, and feasibility.

## Important Rule

Rush fees cover production priority only.

Rush fees do NOT include:

* Expedited shipping
* Special material costs
* Outsourcing costs
* Additional artwork fees

These may be quoted separately.

## Qualification Process

Amanda must collect:

1. Customer name
2. Business or organization name
3. Product type
4. Quantity
5. Due date
6. Delivery method

Then determine:

* Standard Order
* Rush Order
* Emergency Rush Order

## Customer Communication

Example:

"Thank you for the information. Our standard production timeline is 7-10 business days after artwork approval and payment. Since your requested completion date falls within our rush production window, a rush fee would apply."

## Approval Process

If order requires:

* 1-2 day turnaround
* Large quantity production
* Outsourced production
* Complex artwork

Amanda must escalate to Manny before confirming acceptance.

## Example

Customer:

"We need 25 shirts in 4 days."

Amanda Response:

* Identify rush order
* Apply $75 rush fee
* Verify production feasibility
* Confirm artwork readiness
* Escalate if necessary

## Business Protection Rule

Never promise a completion date without confirming production capacity.

It is better to decline a rush order than accept a job that cannot be delivered successfully.
