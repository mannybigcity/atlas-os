# PayPal Invoice Process

## Purpose

Provide a consistent process for creating, sending, tracking, and following up on customer invoices.

## Preferred Platform

PayPal Invoicing

## Step 1 - Verify Order Information

Before creating an invoice, confirm:

* Customer name
* Business name
* Email address
* Phone number
* Products requested
* Quantities
* Due date

## Step 2 - Verify Pricing

Confirm:

* Product pricing
* Decoration pricing
* Setup fees
* Artwork fees
* Rush fees
* Shipping fees
* Sales tax

Never send an invoice until pricing has been verified.

## Step 3 - Create Reference Number

Format:

BUSINESSNAME-MMDDYY

Examples:

* BBHVAC-060826
* CANES-060826
* MANPOWER-060826

Reference numbers help track opportunities and orders.

## Step 4 - Create Invoice

Include:

* Itemized products
* Quantities
* Pricing
* Tax
* Notes
* Payment instructions

## Step 5 - Invoice Notes

Standard reminders:

* Production begins after payment.
* Standard turnaround is 7-10 business days.
* Rush fees may apply.
* Artwork approval may be required.

## Step 6 - Send Invoice

Verify:

* Email address
* Invoice total
* Reference number

Then send.

## Step 7 - Update CRM

Record:

* Invoice date
* Amount
* Customer
* Status

Status options:

* Draft
* Sent
* Viewed
* Paid
* Overdue
* Cancelled

## Step 8 - Follow-Up

24-48 hours after sending:

* Confirm receipt
* Answer questions
* Request decision

## Step 9 - Payment Received

When payment arrives:

* Update status to Paid
* Notify production
* Schedule work

## Kingdom Law

An invoice is not complete until:

1. It is sent.
2. It is tracked.
3. A follow-up is scheduled.
