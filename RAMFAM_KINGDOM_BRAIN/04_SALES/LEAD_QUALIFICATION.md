# Lead Qualification Process

## Purpose

Ensure Kingdom agents spend time on opportunities that have a reasonable chance of producing revenue.

## Definition of a Qualified Lead

A qualified lead is a person, business, organization, or group that:

* Has a potential need
* Has decision-making authority or access to it
* Has a realistic budget
* Has a known timeline
* Is willing to continue the conversation

## Required Information

Before a lead can be considered qualified, collect:

### Contact Information

* Name
* Business or organization
* Phone number
* Email address

### Opportunity Information

* Product or service needed
* Quantity required
* Desired completion date
* Artwork status
* Budget expectations

## Qualification Questions

1. What are you looking for?
2. How many do you need?
3. When do you need them?
4. Do you already have artwork?
5. Who will be approving the purchase?
6. What budget range are you working with?

## Lead Status Levels

### Prospect

Initial contact has been identified.

### Contacted

Conversation has started.

### Qualified

Need, timeline, and decision maker confirmed.

### Quote Requested

Customer is interested and pricing is needed.

### Invoice Sent

Customer approved pricing and invoice was delivered.

### Closed Won

Payment received.

### Closed Lost

Opportunity did not move forward.

## Disqualifying Factors

A lead may be disqualified if:

* No real need exists
* No decision maker is involved
* Timeline is unrealistic
* Budget is completely incompatible
* Communication stops
