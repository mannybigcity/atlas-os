# PLAYBOOK #1 - SIS QUOTE-TO-INVOICE PLAYBOOK

## Purpose

Create a repeatable SIS Custom Creations workflow for turning customer requests into accurate quotes, approved orders, sent invoices, and scheduled follow-ups.

This playbook protects revenue, customer trust, Manny's focus, and production capacity by making sure no quote or invoice moves forward until the required information, pricing, approvals, and follow-up steps are complete.

## Trigger Conditions

Use this playbook when any of the following happens:

* A customer asks for pricing.
* A customer requests shirts, hats, polos, signs, patches, promotional items, laser engraving, event services, or other SIS products/services.
* A returning customer wants to reorder or modify a previous order.
* A prospect from outreach becomes interested enough to request a quote.
* An approved quote is ready to become an invoice.
* An invoice has been sent and needs follow-up.

## Required Information

Before a quote is finalized, collect and verify:

### Customer Information

* Customer name
* Business name, if applicable
* Phone number
* Email address
* Preferred contact method

### Order Information

* Product or service requested
* Quantity
* Sizes, if apparel is involved
* Product colors
* Decoration method, if known
* Artwork/logo/design status
* Artwork file received, if available
* Setup or design help needed, if any
* Shipping, pickup, or delivery requirement

### Timeline Information

Always ask:

"When do you need this completed?"

Standard production time is 7-10 business days after artwork approval and payment receipt.

Rush fee guidance:

* 5-7 business days = $50
* 3-4 business days = $75
* 1-2 business days = $100+ and management review required

Rush fees do not include expedited shipping, outsourcing charges, artwork creation fees, or additional material costs.

### Pricing Information

Verify:

* Product cost
* Decoration cost
* Artwork/setup fee, if applicable
* Rush fee, if applicable
* Shipping or delivery cost, if applicable
* Outsourcing cost, if applicable
* Sales tax
* Final total

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1 - Intake the Request

Record the customer's request and collect the required customer information.

If the request is incomplete, ask follow-up questions before quoting.

### Step 2 - Confirm Product Details

Clarify exactly what the customer wants.

Confirm product type, quantity, sizes, colors, decoration needs, and any special instructions.

### Step 3 - Confirm Artwork Status

Ask whether the customer already has a logo, design, or artwork file.

If artwork exists, request the usable file.

If artwork does not exist or needs cleanup, determine whether a design/setup fee is required.

### Step 4 - Confirm Due Date

Ask when the customer needs the order completed.

Compare the requested due date against standard production time and rush order rules.

Do not promise a timeline until materials, production capacity, artwork status, and payment timing are understood.

### Step 5 - Check Feasibility

Before pricing is finalized, confirm:

* Materials are available or can be sourced in time.
* Equipment and production capacity are available.
* Outsourcing, if needed, still leaves acceptable profit.
* The due date is realistic.
* Quality will not be compromised.

### Step 6 - Build the Quote

Calculate the full quote using verified product, decoration, setup, rush, shipping, outsourcing, and tax information.

The quote should clearly show what is included, the total price, expected timeline, payment requirement, and any limitations.

### Step 7 - Quote Approval Gate

The quote may move forward only after:

* Product details are confirmed.
* Quantity is confirmed.
* Artwork status is confirmed.
* Due date is confirmed.
* Pricing is verified.
* Tax is verified.
* Rush fee is added when required.
* Profitability and feasibility are acceptable.

If any item is missing, the quote remains incomplete.

### Step 8 - Present Quote to Customer

Send or communicate the quote clearly.

Record the date quoted, quoted amount, customer response status, and next follow-up date.

### Step 9 - Customer Approval Gate

Do not create the invoice until the customer approves the quote.

Customer approval must confirm:

* Products/services
* Quantity
* Price
* Timeline
* Artwork/design expectations
* Payment requirement

### Step 10 - Create Reference Number

Create a reference number using this format:

BUSINESSNAME-MMDDYY

Examples:

* BBHVAC-060826
* CANES-060826
* MANPOWER-060826

Use the reference number on invoices, records, and follow-up notes.

### Step 11 - Create the Invoice

Preferred platform: PayPal Invoicing.

Invoice must include:

* Customer name and email
* Reference number
* Itemized products/services
* Quantities
* Pricing
* Sales tax
* Rush fee, if applicable
* Shipping/delivery fee, if applicable
* Payment instructions
* Due date
* Notes about production beginning after payment
* Notes about artwork approval, if required

### Step 12 - Invoice Approval Gate

Before sending the invoice, verify:

* Customer email is correct.
* Invoice total matches approved quote.
* Reference number is correct.
* Tax is included and verified.
* Payment instructions are clear.
* Production notes are included.
* Follow-up date is scheduled.

### Step 13 - Send Invoice

Send the invoice only after the invoice approval gate is complete.

Record the invoice date, amount, customer, reference number, and status.

Recommended invoice statuses:

* Draft
* Sent
* Viewed
* Paid
* Overdue
* Cancelled

### Step 14 - Follow Up

If unpaid, follow up after 24-48 hours.

Follow-up should:

* Confirm receipt.
* Answer questions.
* Remind customer that production begins after payment.
* Request decision or payment.
* Update status and next follow-up date.

### Step 15 - Payment Received

When payment is received:

* Update invoice status to Paid.
* Confirm artwork approval status.
* Notify or schedule production.
* Confirm production timeline.
* Keep customer informed.

## Approval Gates

### Gate 1 - Quote Readiness

A quote is ready only when customer details, product details, quantity, artwork status, due date, pricing, tax, and feasibility are confirmed.

### Gate 2 - Customer Approval

An invoice is created only after the customer approves the quote.

### Gate 3 - Invoice Readiness

An invoice is sent only when the total, tax, reference number, customer email, notes, and payment instructions are verified.

### Gate 4 - Production Readiness

Production begins only after payment receipt and artwork approval, unless Manny explicitly approves an exception.

## Outputs

This playbook should produce:

* Completed customer/order information
* Accurate quote
* Quote status and follow-up date
* Customer approval record
* Reference number
* PayPal invoice
* Invoice status record
* Payment status
* Production handoff after payment and artwork approval

## Exceptions

### Rush Orders

Rush orders require the correct rush fee and must not be accepted if materials, capacity, quality, or profitability are at risk.

### 1-2 Business Day Orders

Any order needed within 1-2 business days requires $100+ rush fee review and management approval.

### Missing Artwork

If artwork is missing, unusable, or needs cleanup, quote design/setup fees and do not begin production until artwork is approved.

### Unverified Pricing

If pricing, tax, shipping, outsourcing, or material costs cannot be verified, do not send the quote or invoice.

### Customer Changes After Quote

If the customer changes quantity, product type, design, due date, or delivery method, revise the quote before invoicing.

### Unpaid Invoice

If the invoice is unpaid after 24-48 hours, follow up and keep the order out of production until payment is received, unless Manny approves otherwise.

### Capacity or Quality Risk

Do not accept orders that would overload production, reduce quality, damage customer trust, or weaken Kingdom stability.

## Kingdom Rule

No quote is complete until:

1. Due date is known.
2. Product and quantity are confirmed.
3. Artwork status is known.
4. Pricing is verified.
5. Tax is verified.
6. Feasibility is confirmed.
7. Follow-up is scheduled.

No invoice is complete until:

1. Customer approval is received.
2. Reference number is created.
3. Invoice is sent.
4. Invoice is tracked.
5. Follow-up is scheduled.

Revenue before expansion. Customer commitments before new opportunities. Protect the King's focus.
