# PLAYBOOK #2 - SIS CUSTOMER ORDER INTAKE PLAYBOOK

## Purpose

Create a consistent SIS Custom Creations intake process for capturing every new customer order request clearly, completely, and accurately before quoting, invoicing, production, or follow-up begins.

This playbook protects revenue, customer commitments, Manny's focus, and production quality by making sure the Kingdom collects the right information at the beginning instead of correcting preventable problems later.

## Trigger Conditions

Use this playbook when any of the following happens:

* A new customer asks about an SIS Custom Creations product or service.
* A returning customer requests a new order or reorder.
* A customer contacts SIS through phone, text, email, marketplace, social media, referral, event, or in person.
* A prospect asks for shirts, hats, polos, signs, laser engraving, promotional products, event services, or custom items.
* A lead becomes serious enough to require pricing, artwork review, timeline review, or production evaluation.
* An existing customer changes an order request before quote or invoice creation.

## Required Information

Before the request is moved to quoting or production review, collect and record the following information.

### Customer Information

* Customer name
* Business or organization name, if applicable
* Phone number
* Email address
* Preferred contact method
* Best time to contact, if needed
* New customer or returning customer status
* Referral source, if known

### Order Request Information

* Product or service requested
* Quantity needed
* Sizes, if apparel is involved
* Product colors
* Decoration method, if known
* Design, logo, wording, or personalization details
* Artwork status
* Artwork file received, if available
* Special instructions
* Budget expectation, if volunteered or appropriate to ask

### Timeline Information

Always ask:

"When do you need the order completed?"

Record:

* Requested completion date
* Event date, if the order is tied to an event
* Whether the timeline may require rush review
* Pickup, delivery, or shipping expectation

Standard production time is 7-10 business days after payment receipt and artwork approval.

Rush fee guidance:

* 5-7 business days = $50
* 3-4 business days = $75
* 1-2 business days = $100+ and management review required

Rush fees do not include expedited shipping, outsourcing, artwork creation, or extra material costs.

### Artwork Information

Record one of the following artwork statuses:

* Artwork received
* Artwork requested
* Artwork needs cleanup
* Artwork needs creation
* Artwork pending customer approval
* No artwork required

If artwork is received, record the file name, file type, and where it is stored.

### Intake Record Information

Each intake record should include:

* Intake date
* Intake source
* Customer name
* Business name, if applicable
* Requested product/service
* Due date
* Current status
* Assigned next action
* Follow-up date
* Notes

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1 - Acknowledge the Customer

Respond promptly and professionally.

Confirm that SIS can help evaluate the request, then move into information gathering.

### Step 2 - Identify the Customer

Collect customer name, business or organization name, phone number, email address, and preferred contact method.

If the customer is returning, check whether the request is connected to a previous order.

### Step 3 - Capture the Request

Ask what the customer needs and record the product or service requested.

Clarify whether the request involves apparel, hats, signs, promotional items, laser engraving, event services, or another custom product.

### Step 4 - Gather Product Details

Collect quantity, sizes, colors, materials, item preferences, decoration method, and special instructions.

If the customer does not know exact details yet, record the unknowns and set the next action to collect them before quoting.

### Step 5 - Confirm Artwork Status

Ask whether the customer already has artwork, a logo, wording, or a design idea.

If artwork exists, request the usable file.

If artwork does not exist or needs work, record that design/setup review may be required.

### Step 6 - Confirm Timeline

Ask when the customer needs the order completed.

Compare the requested completion date against standard production time and rush fee guidance.

Do not promise completion until feasibility is reviewed.

### Step 7 - Confirm Delivery Method

Ask whether the customer expects pickup, local delivery, shipping, event delivery, or another fulfillment method.

Record any location, shipping, or delivery constraints.

### Step 8 - Check Intake Completeness

Review the intake record for missing required information.

If information is missing, ask the customer targeted follow-up questions before moving to quote review.

### Step 9 - Assign Status

Assign one intake status:

* New request
* Waiting on customer information
* Waiting on artwork
* Ready for quote review
* Rush review needed
* Feasibility review needed
* Not a fit
* Converted to quote

### Step 10 - Schedule Follow-Up

Every intake must have a next action and follow-up date.

If waiting on customer information or artwork, follow up within 24-48 hours.

### Step 11 - Handoff to Quote Review

Move the request to the quote-to-invoice process only after the intake approval gate is complete.

Include all collected details, open questions, artwork status, requested due date, delivery expectations, and follow-up notes.

## Approval Gates

### Gate 1 - Customer Identity Complete

The intake may continue only when the customer name and at least one reliable contact method are recorded.

### Gate 2 - Order Request Clear

The request is clear only when the product/service, quantity, due date, artwork status, and fulfillment expectation are known or clearly marked as unknown.

### Gate 3 - Rush/Feasibility Review

Any request inside standard production time, involving unusual products, uncertain materials, outsourcing, large quantities, or high risk must be reviewed before a promise is made.

### Gate 4 - Quote Handoff Readiness

The order may move to quote review only when:

* Customer contact information is recorded.
* Product or service requested is recorded.
* Quantity is recorded.
* Due date is recorded.
* Artwork status is recorded.
* Delivery or pickup expectation is recorded.
* Missing information is documented.
* Next action and follow-up date are assigned.

## Outputs

This playbook should produce:

* Completed SIS customer intake record
* Clear customer contact details
* Clear product/service request
* Quantity, size, color, and decoration details when applicable
* Requested completion date
* Artwork status
* Fulfillment expectation
* Rush or feasibility review flag when needed
* Intake status
* Next action
* Follow-up date
* Quote-ready handoff packet when intake is complete

## Exceptions

### Customer Does Not Know What They Need

Ask guiding questions and record the request as discovery needed. Do not quote until the product, quantity, and timeline are clear enough to price responsibly.

### Missing Contact Information

Do not create a quote record without at least one reliable contact method.

### Missing Due Date

Do not move to quote review until the requested completion date or event date is known.

### Missing Artwork

If artwork is missing, request it and mark the intake as waiting on artwork. If design help is needed, flag design/setup review.

### Rush Timeline

If the customer needs the order faster than standard production time, flag rush review and do not promise the order until capacity, materials, pricing, and quality risk are checked.

### Unclear Quantity or Sizes

If quantity or sizes are missing, record the intake as waiting on customer information. Do not finalize pricing until quantity and size details are known.

### Capacity or Quality Risk

If the request could overload production, reduce quality, or damage customer trust, escalate for review before accepting the order.

### Not a Fit

If SIS cannot serve the request profitably, safely, or with acceptable quality, mark the intake as not a fit and respond professionally.

## Success Metrics

Track the success of this playbook using:

* Number of new customer intake records created
* Percentage of intakes with complete customer contact information
* Percentage of intakes with due date captured
* Percentage of intakes with artwork status captured
* Percentage of intakes converted to quote review
* Average time from first contact to quote-ready handoff
* Number of follow-ups completed within 24-48 hours
* Number of orders delayed by missing information
* Number of rush requests correctly flagged before quoting
* Customer satisfaction and repeat order rate

## Kingdom Rule

No SIS customer order request moves forward until:

1. Customer identity is known.
2. Contact method is recorded.
3. Product or service request is clear.
4. Quantity is known or the missing quantity is documented.
5. Due date is known.
6. Artwork status is known.
7. Fulfillment expectation is known.
8. Next action is assigned.
9. Follow-up is scheduled.

Revenue before expansion. Customer commitments before new opportunities. Protect the King's focus.
