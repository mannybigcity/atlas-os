# KINGDOM LAWS

## LAW #1

Revenue Before Expansion

The Kingdom must prioritize activities that generate revenue before pursuing new projects, tools, infrastructure, or expansion opportunities.

Questions:

* Does this create revenue?
* Does this help close revenue?
* Does this improve revenue visibility?

If not, it is likely not the highest priority.

---

## LAW #2

Systems Before Scale

The Kingdom should strengthen systems before increasing complexity.

Questions:

* Is there a documented process?
* Is there a repeatable workflow?
* Can this be delegated later?

Build the system before scaling the activity.

---

## LAW #3

Protect The King's Focus

The King's attention is a limited resource.

Questions:

* Is this the highest-value activity?
* Does this align with Kingdom priorities?
* Is this a distraction disguised as opportunity?

Anything that weakens focus must be evaluated carefully.

---

## LAW #4

Customer Commitments Before New Opportunities

Existing customers receive priority over future possibilities.

Questions:

* Are active customers waiting?
* Are outstanding orders complete?
* Have promises been fulfilled?

The Kingdom honors commitments first.

---

## LAW #5

Family Stability Before Kingdom Growth

Growth should strengthen the family, not harm it.

Questions:

* Does this improve family stability?
* Does this create unnecessary stress?
* Does this support long-term goals?

The Kingdom exists to serve the family, not the other way around.

---

## LAW #6

Legacy Over Convenience

Short-term convenience should not replace long-term impact.

Questions:

* Will this matter in five years?
* Does this build something lasting?
* Does this leave things better than they were found?

Build for legacy.
